The First Winter Survival Guide

For many Indian students, arriving in Europe means experiencing sub-zero temperatures and snow for the very first time. Proper preparation is the difference between enjoying the snow and freezing.

1. The "Onion Strategy" (Zwiebelprinzip)

Do not buy one massive, bulky jacket. Instead, use the "Onion Strategy"—layering your clothes. This is crucial because while it is -5°C outside, universities and trains are heated to 22°C. If you wear just a t-shirt and a massive jacket, you will sweat indoors.

  • Base Layer: Thermal wear (innerwear). Uniqlo's HEATTECH is highly recommended by students.
  • Middle Layer: A sweater, hoodie, or fleece jacket.
  • Outer Layer: A wind-proof and water-proof jacket. Down jackets (Daunenjacke) are excellent.

2. Where to Shop on a Budget

Do not buy winter clothes in India before coming; European clothes are designed specifically for European winters.

  • Decathlon: The cheapest place for high-quality thermals, gloves, and basic winter jackets.
  • TK Maxx: Great for finding heavily discounted branded winter jackets (like North Face, Columbia) if you are willing to search through the racks.
  • Primark / C&A: Very affordable, though the quality might not last multiple winters.

3. Mental Health & Vitamin D

The hardest part of a European winter is not the cold—it is the darkness. In December, the sun might set by 4:00 PM, and it is often grey and cloudy for weeks.

Defeating the "Winter Blues"

Many Indian students experience Vitamin D deficiency because of their melanin levels and lack of sunlight, leading to extreme lethargy and low moods.

  • Buy Vitamin D3 supplements from DM or Rossmann (they are very cheap).
  • Go outside for a walk during the few hours of daylight, even if it is cold.
  • Stay active in the Community. Socializing is the best cure for winter loneliness!
