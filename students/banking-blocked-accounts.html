Banking & Blocked Accounts

Navigating the German banking system can be tricky. We recommend a simple two-account strategy to easily manage your blocked account payouts and daily expenses.

1. Traditional Bank (For Your Blocked Account)

To receive your monthly payouts from your blocked account (Expatrio, Fintiba, Coracle, etc.), you need a standard German bank account. Germany has several major traditional banks with physical branches:

  • Sparkasse: Very common, with ATMs everywhere. They usually offer free accounts for students, but terms can vary slightly by region (e.g., Stadtsparkasse München vs. Berliner Sparkasse).
  • Commerzbank: A solid national bank that often provides a free checking account (Girokonto) if you have regular monthly income (your blocked account payout usually counts) and free student accounts.
  • Deutsche Bank: Another major national option, sometimes required by older blocked account providers, though less common today.

Recommendation: Open your primary checking account (Girokonto) at Sparkasse or Commerzbank. Connect this account to your blocked account provider so your monthly allowance is deposited here automatically.

2. Online Bank (For Daily Transactions)

While traditional banks are great for security and physical branches, their apps can be clunky, and they often lack English support. For your daily spending, we highly recommend opening a secondary, modern online bank account.

Important Warning regarding N26

Many blogs recommend N26. However, due to German KYC (Know Your Customer) laws, their verification provider often requires Indian citizens to show a physical Residence Permit (Aufenthaltstitel). Since it takes months to get this card, you usually cannot open an N26 account right after arriving with just a passport and visa.

Our Alternative: Revolut or Vivid

Instead of N26, many incoming students have success opening digital accounts with Revolut or Vivid Money, as their verification processes sometimes accept a National Passport + National Visa + Anmeldung. Benefits include:

  • 100% English Support: The app and customer service are fully available in English.
  • Digital First & Mobile Wallet: Instant push notifications, real-time balance updates, and immediate virtual cards for Apple/Google Pay.
  • Free Instant Transfers: Easily split bills with friends.

The "Two Account" Strategy

The best setup for most international students is to combine both worlds:

  1. Have your blocked account money deposited into your Sparkasse or Commerzbank account every month. Use this account to set up automatic standing orders (Daueraufträge) for your rent and health insurance.
  2. Transfer your remaining "fun/living money" instantly to your Revolut/Vivid account.
  3. Use your digital virtual card on your phone for daily groceries, coffee, and shopping.
